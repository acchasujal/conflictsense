# Final Demo Sequence Recommendation

**Date:** 2026-06-11
**Objective:** Maximize judge reaction by sequencing the conflicts to highlight enterprise risk, multi-step reasoning complexity, and human impact.

## Available Conflicts (From Test Corpus)
1. **Anonymity Conflict:** Whistleblower vs. IT Security (Anonymity promised but impossible due to IT logging).
2. **Data Residency Trilemma:** IT Security vs. HR Remote Work vs. DPDP (Remote workers in India violate US-server and data residency laws simultaneously).
3. **Incident Reporting Gap:** IT Security vs. Whistleblower (Reporting timelines clash with FCA regulations).

## Recommended Demo Sequence

### Act 1: The "Surprising & Legally Risky" Hook (Case 1: Anonymity Conflict)
- **Why start here:** It is an immediate, universally understood enterprise problem. Anonymity is a sacred promise in corporate governance; proving it technically impossible grabs the judge's attention instantly.
- **Judging Impact:** High emotional resonance and clear legal liability. It perfectly sets up *why* this tool exists.
- **Narrative:** "The company promised whistleblower anonymity, but IT logs every action. Our agent found this invisible contradiction before an employee was exposed."

### Act 2: The "Executive-Level Complexity" Showcase (Case 2: Data Residency Trilemma)
- **Why sequence second:** Once the hook is set, you need to prove the system handles massive complexity that a human couldn't realistically hold in their head.
- **Judging Impact:** Demonstrates multi-document, multi-step reasoning. It shows the `ImpactAssessor` calculating the exact number of affected employees (34 India-resident remote workers) based on compounding variables.
- **Narrative:** "Now let's look at scale. The agent detects a 3-way contradiction affecting 34 specific remote workers, spanning HR, IT, and international compliance."

### Act 3: The Human Approval Gate (Closing)
- **Why close here:** Do not show the third conflict (Incident Reporting Gap) as a live trace—it risks dragging the demo out. Instead, pivot from the Trilemma directly to the **Approval Gate**.
- **Judging Impact:** Proves the system is "Enterprise Ready." You demonstrate the Teams-style Adaptive Card where a human explicitly reviews the agent's findings and approves or rejects the remediation.
- **Narrative:** "ConflictSense isn't an autonomous loose cannon. Before any action is taken, this rich reasoning trace is routed to a human for final approval."

## Summary for the 2-Minute Pitch
1. Show Anonymity Conflict (Focus on the 'Aha!' moment and the reasoning UI).
2. Show Data Residency Trilemma (Focus on the complexity and scale).
3. Show the Approval Gate (Focus on safety, governance, and the "Copilot Companion" UX).
