# Whistleblower Policy — Nexora Technologies
# Department: Legal & Compliance
# Effective: August 2023
# Classification: INTERNAL — ALL EMPLOYEES

---

## §1. Purpose

Nexora Technologies is committed to conducting business with the highest standards of integrity. This policy exists to provide every employee with a safe, confidential, and protected mechanism to report concerns about unethical conduct, regulatory breaches, or illegal activity without fear of retaliation.

---

## §2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, agency workers, and consultants engaged with Nexora Technologies in any capacity.

---

## §3. What to Report

§3.1 — Employees should report any genuine concern about:
- Financial fraud, accounting irregularities, or misrepresentation
- Bribery, corruption, or conflicts of interest
- Breaches of regulatory obligations (FCA, DPDP, GDPR, etc.)
- Health and safety violations
- Abuse of authority or bullying
- Any other conduct believed to be illegal or harmful

§3.2 — Reports should be made in good faith. Malicious or knowingly false reports are a disciplinary matter.

---

## §4. Reporting Channels

§4.1 — The primary reporting mechanism is the Nexora Ethics Portal (https://ethics.nexora.internal). This platform is operated and maintained by an independent third-party ethics management provider.

§4.2 — Reports filed through the ethics portal are anonymous. Employee identity is never logged or traceable by any internal party. The ethics portal does not capture IP addresses, session tokens, device identifiers, or any metadata that could be used to identify the reporter. All report data is encrypted end-to-end and accessible only to the independent Legal Compliance Committee.

§4.3 — Employees may alternatively contact the Legal & Compliance Committee directly at compliance@nexora.com. This channel is not anonymous.

§4.4 — Reports involving senior leadership (VP and above) must be directed to the Chair of the Audit Committee.

---

## §5. Confidentiality

§5.1 — The identity of a whistleblower who uses the ethics portal will never be disclosed, even in the event of a legal investigation, unless the reporter provides explicit written consent or is compelled by a court order.

§5.2 — Internal parties, including HR, IT Security, and management, have no access to ethics portal reports or reporter identity.

---

## §6. Investigation Process

§6.1 — All reports received via the ethics portal are reviewed by the independent Legal Compliance Committee within 5 business days.

§6.2 — A preliminary assessment will be completed within 10 business days. The reporter (if contact details were provided) will be notified of the outcome.

§6.3 — Investigations involving regulatory matters are coordinated with the General Counsel's office.

---

## §7. Non-Retaliation

§7.1 — Nexora Technologies strictly prohibits retaliation against any employee who makes a good-faith report under this policy.

§7.2 — Retaliation includes: dismissal, demotion, harassment, exclusion, or any other detrimental treatment related to the report.

§7.3 — Any manager found to have retaliated against a whistleblower will be subject to immediate disciplinary action, up to and including termination.

---

## §8. Regulatory Reporting

§8.1 — This policy does not affect an employee's right to report concerns directly to external regulators, including the FCA, ICO, or other relevant authorities.

§8.2 — Employees who are unsure whether a matter should be reported internally or externally are encouraged to seek guidance from the Legal & Compliance team.

---

*Policy Owner: General Counsel / Legal & Compliance Committee*
*Review Cycle: Annual or upon material regulatory change*
