# DPDP Compliance Directive — Nexora Technologies
# Issued by: Sharma & Associates LLP, External Legal Counsel
# Date: May 2026 | Effective: July 1, 2026
# Classification: CONFIDENTIAL — BOARD AND SENIOR LEADERSHIP ONLY

---

## URGENT: REGULATORY COMPLIANCE REQUIRED BY JULY 1, 2026

This directive is issued on behalf of the Board of Nexora Technologies following completion of a mandatory compliance review under India's Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act, 2023.

Non-compliance after the effective date carries penalties of up to ₹250 crore (approximately £23 million) per violation under DPDP Act §91. This is not a theoretical risk. The Data Protection Board of India has signalled active enforcement from Q3 2026.

---

## §1. Regulatory Background

§1.1 — The Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP Act) received Presidential assent on 11 August 2023. Enforcement provisions for data residency and localisation take effect July 1, 2026.

§1.2 — Nexora Technologies employs 34 personnel classified as Indian-resident data principals under the Act. These individuals' personal data — including employment records, payroll data, performance information, and communications metadata — is currently processed on US-domiciled servers.

§1.3 — This processing arrangement is non-compliant with the DPDP Act as of the effective date.

---

## §2. Regulatory Requirements

§2.1 — DPDP Act §23(1): Data fiduciaries processing personal data of Indian-resident data principals must process and store such data exclusively within Indian jurisdiction unless an explicit data localisation exemption has been granted by the Data Protection Board.

§2.2 — No such exemption has been applied for or granted in respect of Nexora Technologies.

§2.3 — DPDP Act §91: Penalties for systemic non-compliance (i.e., structural failure to implement required data localisation) may reach ₹250 crore per violation. The Board has confirmed that each data principal affected constitutes a separate violation.

---

## §3. Required Technical Actions

§3.1 — Personal data of Indian-resident employees must be processed within Indian jurisdiction. Effective July 1, 2026, no personal data of India-based employees may be processed, stored, or transmitted through non-Indian infrastructure without explicit Data Protection Board exemption.

§3.2 — The IT Infrastructure team must provision India-region Azure compute and storage resources (India Central / India South) for all affected data pipelines by June 28, 2026.

§3.3 — The current US East data pipelines must be updated to geo-route Indian-resident employee data to India-region servers. IP allowlisting, VPN routing tables, and backup schedules must be updated accordingly.

§3.4 — A Data Localisation Implementation Plan must be submitted to external counsel by June 20, 2026 for regulatory review.

---

## §4. Required Policy Actions

§4.1 — The IT Security Policy must be amended to carve out Indian-resident employee data processing from the US-only server mandate (current IT Security Policy §4.2). This carve-out must be approved by the CTO and documented in writing.

§4.2 — The HR Remote Work Policy must be reviewed to assess whether permitting India-based employees to work from any global location remains compliant with data residency obligations.

§4.3 — A DPDP-compliant Data Processing Agreement (DPA) must be executed with any third party processing Indian-resident employee data.

---

## §5. Governance and Accountability

§5.1 — The Chief Technology Officer (CTO) is designated as the DPDP Implementation Owner.

§5.2 — Weekly compliance status reports are required from June 7, 2026 through July 1, 2026.

§5.3 — The General Counsel must brief the Board at the next scheduled meeting on the implementation status.

---

## §6. Penalties for Failure

§6.1 — Failure to achieve compliance by July 1, 2026 will result in:
- Mandatory regulatory disclosure to the Data Protection Board of India
- Civil liability for each affected data principal
- Potential criminal referral for senior officers under DPDP Act §96
- Reputational damage in Nexora's Indian business markets
