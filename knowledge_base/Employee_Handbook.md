# Employee Handbook — Nexora Technologies
# Department: Human Resources
# Effective: February 2024
# Classification: INTERNAL — ALL EMPLOYEES

---

Welcome to Nexora Technologies! This handbook is your guide to working life at Nexora. It covers everything from benefits to behaviour, helping you understand what we expect of you and what you can expect from us.

---

## §1. Our Values

At Nexora, we live by four values:

- **Integrity:** We do the right thing, even when no one is watching.
- **Curiosity:** We never stop learning.
- **Accountability:** We own our outcomes.
- **Inclusion:** Everyone belongs here.

---

## §2. Employment Basics

§2.1 — Your employment contract, role description, and compensation package are documented in your offer letter. Please keep a copy for your records.

§2.2 — Your probationary period is 6 months. During this time, your manager will check in with you regularly to ensure you're settling in well.

§2.3 — Any changes to your role, title, or compensation must be confirmed in writing by HR before they take effect.

---

## §3. Working Hours

§3.1 — Standard working hours are 9:00–18:00 with a 1-hour lunch break, Monday–Friday. Flexible working arrangements are available subject to role requirements.

§3.2 — Overtime is not standard. Any sustained requirement for additional hours should be discussed with your manager.

---

## §4. Remote Work

§4.1 — Remote work is available to all eligible employees. Refer to the HR Remote Work Policy for full details.

---

## §5. Leave

§5.1 — Annual leave entitlement: 25 days per year (plus UK public holidays).

§5.2 — Sick leave: Nexora provides full pay for up to 30 days of sick absence per rolling year.

§5.3 — Parental leave: 26 weeks fully paid for primary caregivers, 4 weeks for secondary caregivers.

---

## §6. Benefits

§6.1 — Nexora provides private medical insurance, dental cover, life assurance at 4x salary, and an Employee Assistance Programme (EAP).

§6.2 — The pension scheme provides an employer contribution of 8% for employee contributions of 5% or more.

§6.3 — Employees are eligible for an annual learning & development budget of £1,500.

---

## §7. Code of Conduct

§7.1 — All employees are expected to behave with professionalism and respect in all work-related settings, including remote communication.

§7.2 — Discrimination, harassment, or bullying of any kind is a serious disciplinary offence.

§7.3 — Conflicts of interest must be disclosed to your line manager and HR.

---

## §8. Compensation and Relocation

§8.1 — Salary reviews take place annually in April.

§8.2 — Bonus eligibility is determined by your role band and individual performance rating. Bonuses are discretionary and not guaranteed.

§8.3 — Employees relocated to regional offices are automatically entitled to a standard relocation allowance of $5,000, disbursed in the first payroll cycle following confirmed relocation.

---

## §9. Performance Reviews

§9.1 — Nexora uses a bi-annual performance review cycle: mid-year (July) and end-of-year (December).

§9.2 — Performance ratings range from 1 (Needs Improvement) to 5 (Outstanding).

§9.3 — Employees rated 1 for two consecutive cycles will be placed on a formal Performance Improvement Plan (PIP).

---

## §10. Disciplinary Process

§10.1 — Minor misconduct is addressed through informal discussion with your manager.

§10.2 — Serious misconduct may result in formal disciplinary proceedings, including suspension and termination.

§10.3 — You have the right to be accompanied by a colleague or trade union representative at formal disciplinary hearings.

---

## §11. Grievance Process

§11.1 — If you have a workplace concern, speak to your HR Business Partner in the first instance.

§11.2 — Formal grievance procedures are documented separately and available on the Nexora intranet.

---

## §12. IT and Data

§12.1 — All employees must comply with the IT Security Policy and the Data Governance Policy.

§12.2 — Do not share company information on public platforms or personal storage services (e.g., personal Google Drive, Dropbox).

---

## §13. Confidentiality

§13.1 — All employees sign a confidentiality agreement as part of their employment contract. This obligation survives termination of employment.

---

## §14. Leaving Nexora

§14.1 — Notice periods are specified in your employment contract.

§14.2 — Upon leaving, all company equipment must be returned and all company accounts will be deactivated within 24 hours of your final working day.

---

## §15. Health and Safety

§15.1 — Nexora takes the health and safety of all employees seriously. Report any hazards or near-misses to facilities@nexora.com.

---

## §16. Equality and Diversity

§16.1 — Nexora is an equal opportunities employer. We do not discriminate on the basis of age, disability, gender, race, religion, sexual orientation, or any other protected characteristic.

---

## §17. Environmental Responsibility

§17.1 — Nexora is committed to reducing its environmental footprint. Employees are encouraged to minimise paper usage, reduce travel, and participate in our sustainability initiatives.

---

## §18. Whistleblowing

§18.1 — See the Whistleblower Policy for full details on how to raise concerns. Nexora protects all good-faith reporters from retaliation.

---

## §19. Social Media

§19.1 — Employees must not post content that could be mistaken for an official Nexora statement without Communications team approval.

---

## §20. Records Retention

§20.1 — Nexora retains all historical employee training logs, performance reviews, and compliance records indefinitely to support career tracing and alumni validation.

§20.2 — Employees have no right to request deletion of training completion records, certification logs, or compliance attestations, as these constitute regulatory audit evidence.

---

## §21. Expenses

§21.1 — All business expenses must be submitted via the Nexora Expenses System within 30 days of being incurred with receipts attached.

---

## §22. Emergency Expenses

§22.1 — For emergency travel or critical supply procurement under £100, employees may self-certify the expense using the emergency form if a receipt was unobtainable. This exception does not apply to expenses above £100.
