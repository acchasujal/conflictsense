# Data Governance Policy — Nexora Technologies
# Department: Legal & Compliance
# Effective: November 2023
# Classification: INTERNAL

---

## §1. Purpose and Scope

This policy establishes the principles, standards, and responsibilities governing the collection, use, storage, transfer, and deletion of data across Nexora Technologies. It applies to all data assets — structured and unstructured — regardless of format or storage medium.

---

## §2. Guiding Principles

§2.1 — **Lawfulness:** All data processing must have a lawful basis under applicable law (GDPR, DPDP Act, CCPA, or equivalent).

§2.2 — **Purpose Limitation:** Data collected for one purpose must not be repurposed without explicit review and, where required, consent.

§2.3 — **Accuracy:** Data must be kept accurate and up-to-date. Inaccurate data must be corrected or deleted without delay.

§2.4 — **Integrity and Confidentiality:** Data must be protected against unauthorised access, loss, or destruction through appropriate technical and organisational measures.

---

## §3. Data Minimisation and Retention

§3.1 — In alignment with data minimisation principles, employee personal records and performance evaluations must be destroyed 12 months post-employment. This includes performance reviews, disciplinary records, and training logs, unless retention is required by specific regulatory obligation.

§3.2 — Financial transaction records are subject to the 7-year statutory retention requirement under UK financial regulations and must not be deleted before that period elapses.

§3.3 — Retention schedules must be documented and approved by the Chief Data Officer. Unapproved retention beyond the scheduled period constitutes a policy breach.

---

## §4. Data Subject Rights

§4.1 — Nexora employees are data subjects under GDPR and DPDP. They have the right to access, correct, and request deletion of their personal data.

§4.2 — Data subject requests must be fulfilled within 30 days. Extensions up to 90 days are permitted for complex requests, with written notice to the data subject.

§4.3 — The Data Protection Officer (DPO) is responsible for maintaining a register of all data subject requests and outcomes.

---

## §5. Cross-Border Data Transfers

§5.1 — Transfer of personal data outside the EEA or India requires a legal mechanism approved by the DPO (e.g., Standard Contractual Clauses, Adequacy Decision, or DPDP Board exemption).

§5.2 — Ad hoc cross-border data transfers without an approved mechanism are prohibited and must be reported to the DPO as a potential data breach.

---

## §6. Data Classification

§6.1 — All data assets must be classified per the IT Security Policy §8 framework: Public, Internal, Confidential, or Restricted.

§6.2 — Restricted data may only be processed by individuals with explicit role-based authorisation documented in the access control register.

---

## §7. Third-Party Data Processing

§7.1 — Any third party processing Nexora personal data must execute a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) with Nexora before processing begins.

§7.2 — Processor compliance with this policy must be verified annually through documented vendor assessments.

§7.3 — EU employee data subject to GDPR residency requirements must be processed only within EEA-compliant jurisdictions or under approved transfer mechanisms.

---

## §8. Breach Notification

§8.1 — Any confirmed or suspected data breach must be reported to the DPO within 24 hours of discovery.

§8.2 — The DPO will assess whether the breach requires notification to a supervisory authority (e.g., ICO, Data Protection Board of India) within the statutory window.

---

## §9. Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Chief Data Officer | Policy ownership and strategic oversight |
| Data Protection Officer | Compliance monitoring, DPO obligations, breach coordination |
| Department Heads | Implementing controls within their data domains |
| All Employees | Following classification and handling procedures |

---

## §10. Enforcement

Breaches of this policy will be treated as disciplinary matters. Serious or repeated breaches may result in termination and regulatory referral.

---

## §11. Retention Conflict Resolution

§11.1 — Where this policy's retention periods conflict with requirements of other Nexora policies (e.g., IT Security Policy §12.1 requiring 7-year log retention), the longer retention period applies, subject to DPO approval.

§11.2 — To comply with financial audits, all communications and work products of employees must be retained for a mandatory period of 7 years. This obligation supersedes the standard 12-month personal record destruction rule for documents that also constitute financial or audit records.

---

*Policy Owner: Chief Data Officer*
*Review Cycle: Annual or upon material regulatory change*
