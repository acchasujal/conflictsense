# Demo Moments

## Moment 1: Analysis Begins
- What appears on screen: Landing page, loaded policy set, Run Analysis action.
- What narrator says: `We are checking enterprise policies for contradictions that humans miss when documents live in silos.`
- What judge should think: This is a real enterprise problem, not a toy demo.

## Moment 2: Evidence Retrieved
- What appears on screen: Foundry IQ-driven retrieval and live trace entries.
- What narrator says: `ConflictSense grounds each document before it reasons over the result.`
- What judge should think: The system is trustworthy because it is evidence-led.

## Moment 3: Conflict Candidate Detected
- What appears on screen: First candidate conflict surfaced in the reasoning trace.
- What narrator says: `The system is now comparing policies that cannot both be true.`
- What judge should think: This is more than search; it is structured reasoning.

## Moment 4: Conflict Rejected
- What appears on screen: A weak candidate filtered out by validation.
- What narrator says: `Bad findings do not make it into the dashboard. The validator blocks them.`
- What judge should think: The product is selective, not noisy.

## Moment 5: Anonymity Conflict Validated
- What appears on screen: Whistleblower anonymity vs. IT logging contradiction.
- What narrator says: `Nexora promises anonymity, but its own logging policy makes that impossible.`
- What judge should think: The demo has a memorable, high-stakes core insight.

## Moment 6: Impact Assessment Completed
- What appears on screen: Impact panel listing affected groups and systems.
- What narrator says: `Now we can show who is affected, not just that a conflict exists.`
- What judge should think: This has enterprise value, not just technical novelty.

## Moment 7: Human Approval Gate
- What appears on screen: Approve, reject, escalate controls.
- What narrator says: `ConflictSense stops at the boundary of action and hands the decision to a human.`
- What judge should think: This is Responsible AI, not automation theater.
