# Final Judge Walkthrough

ConflictSense solves a problem enterprises already have but rarely see clearly: policy documents can contradict each other in ways that make compliance promises impossible to satisfy.

Current tools fail because they mostly search, summarize, or chat. They do not reliably compare one policy against another, preserve source-level grounding, or show a human-readable reasoning path that a judge or compliance officer can audit.

Foundry IQ matters because it keeps every claim tied to specific source documents and passages. That makes the output defensible instead of speculative.

Multiple agents matter because the task is not a single question. It is a workflow: retrieve evidence, compare statements, validate the finding, and assess impact. Each step is narrower, clearer, and easier to trust than one giant model response.

Human approval matters because enterprise risk should not be acted on automatically. ConflictSense surfaces the contradiction, then stops and asks a person to decide what happens next.

ConflictSense is different because it is built around a visible reasoning trace, grounded citations, and a governed approval boundary. It does not try to replace enterprise judgment. It helps the enterprise prove when its own policies disagree.

In one sentence: ConflictSense turns hidden policy contradictions into auditable, human-reviewed enterprise findings.
