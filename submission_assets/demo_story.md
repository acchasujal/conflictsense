# ConflictSense Demo Story

## Core Narrative
Nexora Technologies tells employees they can report harassment anonymously. That promise is false. Their IT Security Policy logs every system action, which means anonymity is structurally impossible.

## Why This Story Works
- It is easy to remember.
- It is legally and operationally meaningful.
- It is a contradiction humans can understand instantly.
- It demonstrates why surface-level search is not enough.
- It creates a strong emotional contrast between "promised privacy" and "logged reality."

## 90-Second Story Arc
### 1. Set the stakes
We open on a crowded enterprise knowledge base and explain that policies are written in silos.

### 2. Start the analysis
We run ConflictSense and show the live SSE reasoning trace.

### 3. Reveal the contradiction
The Whistleblower Policy promises anonymity, but the IT Security Policy requires identity logging.

### 4. Prove the grounding
We hover citations and show that every claim is tied to source text through Foundry IQ.

### 5. Show responsible AI
We stop at the Approval Gate and route the finding to a human instead of pretending the system can act on its own.

## Suggested Spoken Line
"ConflictSense finds structural impossibilities across enterprise policy. In this case, Nexora promises anonymous reporting, but its own logging policy makes anonymity impossible."

## Why Judges Should Care
This is not a toy demo for spotting similar words.
It is a product story about reducing compliance risk, increasing auditability, and helping enterprises catch contradictions before they become incidents.
