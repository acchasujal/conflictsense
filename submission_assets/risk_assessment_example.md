# Risk Assessment Example

## Hero Conflict
Whistleblower Anonymity vs. IT Identity Logging

## Validated Conflict
The Whistleblower Policy promises that ethics portal reports are anonymous and not traceable by internal parties.
The IT Security Policy requires every system access event to be logged with full user identity and no exceptions.

## Impact
The conflict affects whistleblower reports submitted through the ethics portal. The system can no longer treat anonymity as only an HR or legal promise because the technical logging control directly affects whether the promise can be fulfilled.

## Risk Assessment
- Risk level: CRITICAL
- Risk categories: Compliance Risk, Legal Risk, Reputational Risk, Security Risk, Governance Risk
- Risk score: Evidence-weighted score derived from validated severity, citation coverage, risk categories, and confidence.
- Confidence: Bounded by the underlying conflict confidence and citation confidence.

## Grounded Reasoning
This conflict creates a situation where Nexora promises anonymous reporting while maintaining mandatory identity logging. The promise cannot be operationally fulfilled. That creates legal and governance risk because the written policy commitment conflicts with the technical control, and reputational risk because employee trust in reporting channels may be compromised.

## What Is Not Claimed
- No financial penalty is invented.
- No employee count is invented.
- No regulatory fine is asserted unless it appears in grounded source evidence.
- If exact exposure is unavailable, the assessment states uncertainty rather than guessing.

## Why Judges Should Care
RiskQuantifier turns the demo from "we found a contradiction" into "we found a contradiction that leadership can act on." It connects evidence, impact, and enterprise risk without overstating what the system knows.
